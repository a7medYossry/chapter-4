Dialogue 1 - الْحِوَارُ الأَوَّلُ

Between طَارِق (Tariq) and طَاهِر (Tahir)

طَارِق: مَتَى تَسْتَيْقِظُ؟

Tariq: When do you wake up?

طَاهِر: أَسْتَيْقِظُ عِنْدَ الْفَجْرِ.

Tahir: I wake up at dawn.

طَارِق: أَيْنَ تُصَلِّي الْفَجْرَ؟

Tariq: Where do you pray Fajr?

طَاهِر: أُصَلِّي الْفَجْرَ فِي الْمَسْجِدِ.

Tahir: I pray Fajr in the mosque.

طَارِق: هَلْ تَنَامُ بَعْدَ الصَّلاةِ؟

Tariq: Do you sleep after the prayer?

طَاهِر: لا، لا أَنَامُ بَعْدَ الصَّلاةِ.

Tahir: No, I do not sleep after the prayer.

Clarification:The first "لا" means "No." The second "لا" negates the present tense verb "أَنَامُ" (I sleep) to mean "I do not sleep."

طَارِق: مَاذَا تَفْعَلُ بَعْدَ الصَّلاةِ؟

Tariq: What do you do after the prayer?

طَاهِر: أَقْرَأُ الْقُرْآنَ.

Tahir: I read the Quran.

طَارِق: وَمَتَى تَذْهَبُ إِلَى الْمَدْرَسَةِ؟

Tariq: And when do you go to school?

طَاهِر: أَذْهَبُ السَّاعَةَ السَّابِعَةَ.

Tahir: I go at seven o'clock.

طَارِق: هَلْ تَذْهَبُ بِالسَّيَّارَةِ؟

Tariq: Do you go by car?

طَاهِر: لا، أَذْهَبُ بِالْحَافِلَةِ.

Tahir: No, I go by bus.

More on Telling Time - الْمَزِيدُ عَنِ السَّاعَة

  • كَمِ السَّاعَةُ؟

    What's the time?

  • السَّاعَةُ الثَّالِثَةُ وَ ... / إِلَّا ...

    The time is three and (past) ... / except (to) ...

  • نِصْف = half

  • رُبْع = quarter

  • ثُلُث = one third (20 minutes)

  • دَقِيقَة (ج. دَقَائِق) = minute (pl. minutes)

  • ثَانِيَة (ج. ثَوَانٍ) = second (pl. seconds)

  • السَّاعَةُ الْوَاحِدَةُ وَالرُّبْعُ. (1:15 - One and a quarter / Quarter past one.)

  • السَّاعَةُ الثَّانِيَةُ وَالنِّصْفُ. (2:30 - Two and a half / Half past two.)

  • السَّاعَةُ الثَّالِثَةُ إِلَّا رُبْعًا. (2:45 - Three except a quarter / Quarter to three.)

  • السَّاعَةُ الْخَامِسَةُ إِلَّا خَمْسَ دَقَائِقَ. (4:55 - Five except five minutes / Five to five.)

فِعْل الْأَمْر (The Command Verb)

The command verb (فِعْل الْأَمْر - Fi'l al-Amr) in Arabic is used to give orders or make requests. It's a fundamental part of the language, and understanding how to form it is crucial for communication. The command verb is closely related to and derived from the present/future tense verb (الْفِعْل الْمُضَارِع - al-Fi'l al-Muḍāriʿ).

Important Rule

Before we begin, there's a vital rule in Arabic phonology:

"A word can never begin with a letter that's sakin (has a sukoon)."

Clarification: The first letter of a word must have a vowel (i.e., it has a Fatḥah ـَـ, a Ḍammah ـُـ, or a Kasrah ـِـ). It cannot start with a Sukoon ـْـ (which indicates the absence of a vowel).

How to Derive the Command Verb from the Present Tense Verb (for the Masculine Singular Addressee - أَنْتَ - you, masculine singular)

To form the command verb for a singular masculine person you are speaking to (أَنْتَ - anta), follow these steps, starting with the present tense verb (المضارع - al-Muḍāriʿ), usually in its "he" form:

Step 1: Remove the Present Tense Prefix

The present tense verb (Mudaari') begins with one of the four present tense prefix letters (ء/أ, ن, ي, ت). To get the command, we remove this prefix (typically the 'يـ' when starting from the base "he" form of the Mudaari').

  • يَقْرَأُ (yaqra'u - he reads) -> قْرَأُ (qra'u)
  • يَكْتُبُ (yaktubu - he writes) -> كْتُبُ (ktubu)
  • يَغْسِلُ (yaghsilu - he washes) -> غْسِلُ (ghsilu)
  • يَجْلِسُ (yajlisu - he sits) -> جْلِسُ (jlisu)
  • يَتَوَضَّأُ (yatawaḍḍa'u - he performs ablution) -> تَوَضَّأُ (tawaḍḍa'u)
Step 2: Dealing with an Initial Sukoon

If the first letter of the remaining word is sakin (has a Sukoon), we add a "connecting hamzah" (همزة الوصل - hamzat al-waṣl), written as an Alif (ا), at the beginning to enable pronunciation.

  • قْرَأُ (qra'u) -> (starts with sukoon on Qaf) -> ا + قْرَأُ
  • كْتُبُ (ktubu) -> (starts with sukoon on Kaf) -> ا + كْتُبُ
  • غْسِلُ (ghsilu) -> (starts with sukoon on Ghayn) -> ا + غْسِلُ
  • جْلِسُ (jlisu) -> (starts with sukoon on Jeem) -> ا + جْلِسُ
  • تَوَضَّأُ (tawaḍḍa'u) -> The 'ت' has a Fatḥah, so no Hamzat al-Waṣl is needed here.
Step 3: Determining the Vowel of Hamzat al-Waṣl

The vowel on the Hamzat al-Waṣl (the Alif: ا) depends on the vowel of the third letter of the original three-letter root of the verb (this is usually the second letter of the stem *after* removing the Mudaari' prefix and *before* adding the Hamzat al-Wasl).

  • If the third root letter has a ḍammah (ـُـ) in the Mudaari' form, the Hamzat al-Waṣl takes a ḍammah.

    يَكْتُبُ (yaktubu). The 'ت' has a ḍammah. So, the command starts with اُ (u). -> اُكْتُبْ (uktub).

  • If the third root letter has a fatḥah (ـَـ) or a kasrah (ـِـ) in the Mudaari' form, the Hamzat al-Waṣl takes a kasrah.

    يَقْرَأُ (yaqra'u). The 'ر' has a fatḥah. So, the command starts with اِ (i). -> اِقْرَأْ (iqra').

    يَغْسِلُ (yaghsilu). The 'س' has a kasrah. So, the command starts with اِ (i). -> اِغْسِلْ (ighsil).

    يَجْلِسُ (yajlisu). The 'ل' has a kasrah. So, the command starts with اِ (i). -> اِجْلِسْ (ijlis).

Step 4: Put a Sukoon on the End of the Verb

The command verb for a singular masculine addressee usually ends with a Sukoon (ـْـ).

Final Command Verbs (for singular masculine):

Mudaari' (Present) Command - Masculine English (Command)
يَقْرَأُ (yaqra'u) اِقْرَأْ (Iqra') Read!
يَكْتُبُ (yaktubu) اُكْتُبْ (Uktub) Write!
يَغْسِلُ (yaghsilu) اِغْسِلْ (Ighsil) Wash!
يَجْلِسُ (yajlisu) اِجْلِسْ (Ijlis) Sit!
يَتَوَضَّأُ (yatawaḍḍa'u) تَوَضَّأْ (Tawaḍḍa') Perform ablution!

Command Form for the Feminine Singular Addressee (أَنْتِ - anti - you, feminine singular)

To command a singular female, add Yā' al-Mukhāṭabah (ياء المخاطبة - the 'yaa' of addressing a female) to the end of the masculine command form. The letter preceding this 'ي' typically takes a Kasrah.

English أَنَا (I) نَحْنُ (We) أَنْتَ (You, m.) أَنْتِ (You, f.) هُوَ (He) هِيَ (She)
to do فَعَلْتُ فَعَلْنَا فَعَلْتَ فَعَلْتِ فَعَلَ فَعَلَتْ
to read قَرَأْتُ قَرَأْنَا قَرَأْتَ قَرَأْتِ قَرَأَ قَرَأَتْ
to write كَتَبْتُ كَتَبْنَا كَتَبْتَ كَتَبْتِ كَتَبَ كَتَبَتْ
to go ذَهَبْتُ ذَهَبْنَا ذَهَبْتَ ذَهَبْتِ ذَهَبَ ذَهَبَتْ

Summary of Key Rules

  1. Start with the present tense verb - "he" form.
  2. Remove the present tense prefix - usually 'يـ'.
  3. If the resulting verb starts with a sukoon, add Hamzat al-Waṣl (ا).
    • The Hamzat al-Waṣl takes a ḍammah (اُ) if the third root letter of the verb has a ḍammah in the Mudaari'.
    • Otherwise, the Hamzat al-Waṣl takes a kasrah (اِ).
  4. Make the last letter of the verb sakin (ـْـ) for the singular masculine command.
  5. For the singular feminine command, add Yā' al-Mukhāṭabah (ي) to the end - usually with a kasrah on the preceding letter.

This framework will help you form command verbs for a wide range of common Arabic verbs. Practice is key to mastering these transformations!

Dialogue 1 - الْحِوَارُ الْأَوَّلُ

Between قَاسِم (Qasim) and سَالِم (Salim) - بَيْنَ قَاسِمٍ وَسَالِمٍ

قَاسِم: أَيْنَ تَذْهَبُ يَا سَالِمُ؟

Qasim: Where are you going, O Salim?

سَالِم: إِلَى الْمَطْعَمِ.

Salim: To the restaurant.

قَاسِم: كَمْ وَجْبَةً تَأْكُلُ فِي الْيَوْمِ؟

Qasim: How many meals do you eat in a day?

سَالِم: آكُلُ ثَلَاثَ وَجَبَاتٍ: الْفَطُورَ، وَالْغَدَاءَ، وَالْعَشَاءَ.

Salim: I eat three meals: breakfast, lunch, and dinner.

قَاسِم: هَذَا كَثِيرٌ جِدًّا. أَنَا آكُلُ وَجْبَةً وَاحِدَةً.

Qasim: This is very much. I eat one meal.

سَالِم: هَذَا قَلِيلٌ جِدًّا.

Salim: This is very little.

قَاسِم: مَاذَا تَأْكُلُ فِي الْغَدَاءِ؟

Qasim: What do you eat for lunch?

سَالِم: آكُلُ الْأَرُزَّ وَالْخُبْزَ. وَمَاذَا تَأْكُلُ أَنْتَ؟

Salim: I eat rice and bread. And what do you eat?

Dialogue 3 - الْحِوَارُ الثَّالِثُ

Between the Husband and the Wife - بَيْنَ الزَّوْجِ وَالزَّوْجَةِ

الزَّوْج: السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكُمْ.

The Husband: Peace be upon you.

الزَّوْجَة: وَعَلَيْكُمُ السَّلَامُ.

The Wife: And upon you be peace.

الزَّوْج: أَنَا جَوْعَانُ جِدًّا.

The Husband: I am very hungry.

الزَّوْجَة: الْغَدَاءُ عَلَى الْمَائِدَةِ.

The Wife: Lunch is on the table.

الزَّوْج: مَا هَذَا؟! سَمَكٌ وَلَحْمٌ وَدَجَاجٌ وَأَرُزٌّ وَسَلَطَةٌ وَفَاكِهَةٌ! هَذَا كَثِيرٌ جِدًّا.

The Husband: What is this?! Fish, meat, chicken, rice, salad, and fruit! This is too much.

الزَّوْجَة: لَا تَأْكُلْ. اِجْلِسْ.

The Wife: Don't eat. Sit down.

الزَّوْج: لِمَاذَا؟ أَنَا جَوْعَانُ.

The Husband: Why? I am hungry.

الزَّوْجَة: لَدَيْنَا ضُيُوفٌ.

The Wife: We have guests.

الزَّوْج: لَدَيْنَا ضُيُوفٌ! مَنْ؟

The Husband: We have guests! Who?

الزَّوْجَة: وَالِدِي وَوَالِدَتِي وَأَخِي.

The Wife: My father, my mother, and my brother.

الزَّوْج: أَيْنَ الضُّيُوفُ؟

The Husband: Where are the guests?

الزَّوْجَة: فِي غُرْفَةِ الْجُلُوسِ.

The Wife: In the living room.

  • جَوْعَانُ - Hungry (Opposite: شَبْعَانُ - Full)

  • عَطْشَانُ - Thirsty (Opposite: رَيَّانُ - Quenched)

  • الْمَائِدَة - A table with food on it (vs. طَاوِلَة - any table)

  • ضَيْف (ج. ضُيُوف) - Guest (pl. Guests)

  • لَدَيْنَا - We have

  • لَحْم - Meat

  • دَجَاج - Chicken

  • وَالِدَة - Mother (like أُمّ)

  • أَخ - Brother

  • غُرْفَةُ الْجُلُوسِ - Living room

The Sixth Unit: The Prayer

Dialogue 1 - الْحِوَارُ الأَوَّلُ

Between مُصْطَفَى (Mustafa) and قُصَيّ (Qusay)

مُصْطَفَى: أَيْنَ تُصَلِّي الصَّلَوَاتِ الْخَمْس؟

Mustafa: Where do you pray the five prayers?

قُصَيّ: أُصَلِّي الظُّهْرَ، وَالْعَصْرَ، وَالْمَغْرِبَ، وَالْعِشَاءَ فِي مَسْجِدِ بِلَال.

Qusay: I pray Dhuhr, Asr, Maghrib, and Isha in the mosque of Bilal.

مُصْطَفَى: وَأَيْنَ تُصَل'ِي الْفَجْر؟

Mustafa: And where do you pray Fajr?

قُصَيّ: أُصَلِّي الْفَجْرَ فِي الْبَيْت.

Qusay: I pray Fajr at home.

مُصْطَفَى: لِمَاذَا تُصَلِّي الْفَجْرَ فِي الْبَيْت؟

Mustafa: Why do you pray Fajr at home?

قُصَيّ: لَا أَسْمَعُ الْأَذَان.

Qusay: I don't hear the Adhan.

مُصْطَفَى: هَلْ تَسْتَيْقِظُ مُتَأَخِّراً؟

Mustafa: Do you wake up late?

قُصَيّ: نَعَمْ، بَعْدَ صَلَاةِ الْفَجْر.

Qusay: Yes, after the Fajr prayer.

مُصْطَفَى: اِسْتَيْقِظْ مُبَكِّراً.

Mustafa: Wake up early.

قُصَيّ: لَا أَسْتَطِيع، أَعْمَلُ فِي اللَّيْل.

Qusay: I can't, I work at night.

مُصْطَفَى: ضَعِ الْمُنَبِّهَ بِجَانِبِك.

Mustafa: Put the alarm clock next to you.

قُصَيّ: هَذِهِ فِكْرَةٌ طَيِّبَة. جَزَاكَ اللهُ خَيْراً.

Qusay: This is a good idea. May Allah reward you with good.

مُفْرَدَاتٌ وَمُلَاحَظَاتٌ

  • يَسْمَعُ - To hear. (لَا أَسْمَعُ: I don't hear).

  • يَسْتَطِيعُ - To be able to (can). (لَا أَسْتَطِيعُ: I can't).

  • يَضَعُ - To put. (فعل الأمر: ضَعْ - Put!).

  • مُنَبِّه - Alarm clock.

  • بِجَانِبِكَ - Next to you / By your side.

  • فِكْرَة - Idea.

  • طَيِّبَة - Good. (عكسها: خَبِيثَة - bad/foul. الكلمة الأكثر شيوعاً لـ "bad" هي سَيِّئَة).

  • اللَّيْل - Night. (عكسها: النَّهَار - Day).

Dialogue 2 - الْحِوَارُ الثَّانِي

Between عصام (Isam) and صالح (Salih)

عصام: السَّلامُ عَلَيْكُم.

Isam: Peace be upon you.

صالح: وَعَلَيْكُمُ السَّلام.

Salih: And upon you be peace.

عصام: أَنَا مُسَافِرٌ إِلَى مَكَّةَ. إِلَى أَيْنَ أَنْتَ مُسَافِر؟

Isam: Why are you traveling to Makkah?

عصام: لِلصَّلاةِ فِي الْمَسْجِدِ النَّبَوِيِّ. أَيْنَ سَتُصَلِّي الْجُمُعَة؟

Isam: To pray in the Prophet's Mosque. Where will you pray Jumu'ah?

صالح: فِي الْمَسْجِدِ الْحَرَام، إِنْ شَاءَ الله. وَأَيْنَ سَتُصَلِّي الْجُمُعَة؟

عصام: فِي الْمَسْجِدِ النَّبَوِيِّ، إِنْ شَاءَ الله.

Isam: In the Prophet's Mosque, if Allah wills.

صالح: كَيْفَ سَتُسَافِرُ إِلَى الْمَدِينَة؟

Salih: How will you travel to Madinah?

عصام: أَرْكَبُ الطَّائِرَة.

The Seventh Unit: Studying

Dialogue 1 - الْحِوَارُ الأَوَّلُ

Between غَانِم (Ghanim) and غَالِب (Ghalib)

غانم: اُنْظُرْ إِلَى اللَّوْحَةِ. اِقْرَأ الْجَدْوَلَ الدِّرَاسِيَّ.

Ghalib: Studying is five days a week.

غانم: نَعَمْ، يَوْمُ السَّبْتِ، وَيَوْمُ الْأَحَدِ، وَيَوْمُ الْاِثْنَيْنِ، وَيَوْمُ الثُّلَاثَاءِ، وَيَوْمُ الْأَرْبِعَاءِ.

The Arabic Past Tense Verb (الْفِعْلُ الْمَاضِي)

In Arabic, verbs are the engine of the language, and they are categorized by time into three main types: the present tense (المضارع), the command (الأمر), and the past tense (الماضي). The past tense, or Al-Fi'l al-Māḍī, is often the first one learners master because of its regular patterns and its essential role in narrating events and everyday conversation.

This guide will walk you through everything you need to know to identify, understand, and use the Arabic past tense verb confidently.

How to Identify the Past Tense Verb

Two simple features distinguish the past tense verb from its present tense counterpart:

  1. Structure and Length:

    The past tense verb is typically one letter shorter than the present tense. This is because the present tense always begins with a prefix (أ, ن, ي, ت) to indicate the doer, while the past tense does not have these prefixes.

    Example:
    Present: يَكْتُبُ (yaktubu - he writes)
    Past: كَتَبَ (kataba - he wrote)

  2. The Default Vowel Ending:

    The base form of a past tense verb (which corresponds to the pronoun "He") always ends with a fatḥa (ـَ). In contrast, the base form of a present tense verb ends with a ḍamma (ـُ).

The Past Tense (Al-Māḍī) The Present Tense (Al-Muḍāriʿ) Meaning (Past)
فَعَلَ (faʿala) يَفْعَلُ (yafʿalu) He did
قَرَأَ (qaraʾa) يَقْرَأُ (yaqraʾu) He read
ذَهَبَ (dhahaba) يَذْهَبُ (yadhhabu) He went

Conjugating the Past Tense Verb: Adding Suffixes

The key to using the past tense is understanding that it changes at the end, not the beginning. To show who performed the action, we attach a specific pronoun suffix to the end of the verb.

The Golden Rule of Conjugation:

Before adding the suffix for most pronouns, the final letter of the original verb root takes a sukūn (ـْ), making it silent.

Let's use the verb فَعَلَ (faʿala - he did) as our model.

  • أَنَا (I) → Add the suffix ـتُ (tu)
    فَعَلَ → فَعَلْـ + ـتُ = فَعَلْتُ (faʿaltu - I did)
  • نَحْنُ (We) → Add the suffix ـنَا (nā)
    فَعَلَ → فَعَلْـ + ـنَا = فَعَلْنَا (faʿalnā - We did)
  • أَنْتَ (You, masculine) → Add the suffix ـتَ (ta)
    فَعَلَ → فَعَلْـ + ـتَ = فَعَلْتَ (faʿalta - You did)
  • أَنْتِ (You, feminine) → Add the suffix ـتِ (ti)
    فَعَلَ → فَعَلْـ + ـتِ = فَعَلْتِ (faʿalti - You did)
  • هُوَ (He) → No change. This is the base form.
    فَعَلَ (faʿala - He did)
  • هِيَ (She) → Add the suffix ـتْ (t). This is تَاءُ التَّأْنِيثِ. The letter before it keeps its fatḥa.
    فَعَلَ + ـتْ = فَعَلَتْ (faʿalat - She did)

    Pro Tip: When the verb فَعَلَتْ is followed by a word starting with "Al-" (ال), the sukūn on the تْ changes to a kasra (ـِ) to make pronunciation smoother. e.g., فَعَلَتِ الْبِنْتُ.

How to Practice

The best way to master the past tense is to use it. Start talking about your day. Ask yourself:

"مَاذَا فَعَلْتُ الْيَوْم؟"

(Mādhā faʿaltu l-yawm? - What did I do today?)

Then, try to answer using the patterns you've learned:

  • "I drank coffee." → شَرِبْتُ قَهْوَةً. (Sharibtu qahwatan.)
  • "We read the book." → قَرَأْنَا الْكِتَاب. (Qaraʾnā al-kitāb.)
  • "You (f.) ate the food." → أَنْتِ أَكَلْتِ الطَّعَام. (Anti akalti ṭ-ṭaʿām.)

By consistently applying these rules, you will build a strong foundation for speaking and understanding Arabic narrative and conversation.
