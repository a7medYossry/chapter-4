Dialogue 1 - الْحِوَارُ الأَوَّلُ

Between طَارِق (Tariq) and طَاهِر (Tahir)

طَارِق: مَتَى تَسْتَيْقِظُ؟

Tariq: When do you wake up?

طَاهِر: أَسْتَيْقِظُ عِنْدَ الْفَجْرِ.

Tahir: I wake up at dawn.

طَارِق: أَيْنَ تُصَلِّي الْفَجْرَ؟

Tariq: Where do you pray Fajr?

طَاهِر: أُصَلِّي الْفَجْرَ فِي الْمَسْجِدِ.

Tahir: I pray Fajr in the mosque.

طَارِق: هَلْ تَنَامُ بَعْدَ الصَّلاةِ؟

Tariq: Do you sleep after the prayer?

طَاهِر: لا، لا أَنَامُ بَعْدَ الصَّلاةِ.

Tahir: No, I do not sleep after the prayer.

* (Clarification: The first "لا" means "No." The second "لا" negates the present tense verb "أَنَامُ" (I sleep) to mean "I do not sleep.")

طَارِق: مَاذَا تَفْعَلُ بَعْدَ الصَّلاةِ؟

Tariq: What do you do after the prayer?

طَاهِر: أَقْرَأُ الْقُرْآنَ.

Tahir: I read the Quran.

طَارِق: وَمَتَى تَذْهَبُ إِلَى الْمَدْرَسَةِ؟

Tariq: And when do you go to school?

طَاهِر: أَذْهَبُ السَّاعَةَ السَّابِعَةَ.

Tahir: I go at seven o'clock.

طَارِق: هَلْ تَذْهَبُ بِالسَّيَّارَةِ؟

Tariq: Do you go by car?

طَاهِر: لا، أَذْهَبُ بِالْحَافِلَةِ.

Tahir: No, I go by bus.

Dialogue 2 - الْحِوَارُ الثَّانِي

Between family members - بَيْنَ أَفْرَادِ الأُسْرَةِ

الأُمُّ: هَذَا يَوْمُ الْعُطْلَةِ.

The Mother: This is the day off (holiday).

الأَبُ: هَذَا يَوْمُ الْعَمَلِ.

The Father: This is the work day.

الأُمُّ: مَاذَا سَتَفْعَلُ يَا طَارِقُ؟

The Mother: What will you do, O Tariq?

طَارِقٌ: سَأَكْنُسُ غُرْفَةَ الْجُلُوسِ.

Tariq: I will sweep the living room.

الأُمُّ: وَمَاذَا سَتَفْعَلِينَ يَا فَاطِمَةُ؟

The Mother: And what will you (f.) do, O Fatimah?

فَاطِمَةُ: سَأَكْنُسُ غُرْفَةَ النَّوْمِ.

Fatimah: I will sweep the bed room.

الأُمُّ: وَمَاذَا سَتَفْعَلُ يَا أَحْمَدُ؟

The Mother: And what will you do, O Ahmad?

أَحْمَدُ: سَأَغْسِلُ الْمَلابِسَ.

Ahmad: I will wash the clothes.

الأُمُّ: وَمَاذَا سَتَفْعَلِينَ يَا لَطِيفَةُ؟

The Mother: And what will you (f.) do, O Latifah?

لَطِيفَةُ: سَأَكْوِي الْمَلابِسَ.

Latifah: I will iron the clothes.

الْجَدَّةُ: أَنَا سَأَغْسِلُ الأَطْبَاقَ.

The Grandmother: I will wash the dishes.

الْجَدُّ: وَأَنَا سَأَقْرَأُ الْقُرْآنَ.

The Grandfather: And I will read the Quran.

Dialogue 3 - الْحِوَارُ الثَّالِثُ

Between عَادِل (Adil) and فَيْصَل (Faisal) - بَيْنَ عَادِلٍ وَفَيْصَلٍ

عَادِل: مَتَى تَسْتَيْقِظُ يَوْمَ الْعُطْلَةِ؟

Adil: When do you wake up on the holiday/day off?

فَيْصَل: أَسْتَيْقِظُ مُبَكِّرًا.

Faisal: I wake up early.

فَيْصَل: وَمَتَى تَسْتَيْقِظُ أَنْتَ؟

Faisal: And when do you wake up?

عَادِل: أَسْتَيْقِظُ مُتَأَخِّرًا.

Adil: I wake up late.

فَيْصَل: مَاذَا تَفْعَلُ فِي الصَّبَاحِ؟

Faisal: What do you do in the morning?

عَادِل: أُشَاهِدُ التِّلْفَازَ.

Adil: I watch the television.

عَادِل: وَمَاذَا تَفْعَلُ أَنْتَ؟

Adil: And what do you do?

فَيْصَل: أَقْرَأُ صَحِيفَةً أَوْ كِتَابًا.

Faisal: I read a newspaper or a book.

عَادِل: أَيْنَ تُصَلِّي الْجُمُعَةَ؟

Adil: Where do you pray the Jumu'ah?

فَيْصَل: أُصَلِّي الْجُمُعَةَ فِي الْمَسْجِدِ الْكَبِيرِ.

Faisal: I pray Jumu'ah in the big mosque.

فَيْصَل: وَأَيْنَ تُصَلِّى أَنْتَ؟

Faisal: And where do you pray?

عَادِل: أُصَلِّي فِي الْمَسْجِدِ الْكَبِيرِ أَيْضًا.

Adil: I pray in the big mosque as well.

More on Telling Time - الْمَزِيدُ عَنِ السَّاعَة

  • كَمِ السَّاعَةُ؟

    What's the time?

  • السَّاعَةُ الثَّالِثَةُ وَ ... / إِلَّا ...

    The time is three and (past) ... / except (to) ...

  • نِصْف = half

  • رُبْع = quarter

  • ثُلُث = one third (20 minutes)

  • دَقِيقَة (ج. دَقَائِق) = minute (pl. minutes)

  • ثَانِيَة (ج. ثَوَانٍ) = second (pl. seconds)

Examples - أَمْثِلَة:

  • السَّاعَةُ الْوَاحِدَةُ وَالرُّبْعُ. (1:15 - One and a quarter / Quarter past one.)

  • السَّاعَةُ الثَّانِيَةُ وَالنِّصْفُ. (2:30 - Two and a half / Half past two.)

  • السَّاعَةُ الثَّالِثَةُ إِلَّا رُبْعًا. (2:45 - Three except a quarter / Quarter to three.)

  • السَّاعَةُ الرَّابِعَةُ وَعَشْرُ دَقَائِقَ. (4:10 - Four and ten minutes / Ten past four.)

  • السَّاعَةُ الْخَامِسَةُ إِلَّا خَمْسَ دَقَائِقَ. (4:55 - Five except five minutes / Five to five.)

فِعْل الْأَمْر (The Command Verb)

The command verb (فِعْل الْأَمْر - Fi'l al-Amr) in Arabic is used to give orders or make requests. It's a fundamental part of the language, and understanding how to form it is crucial for communication. The command verb is closely related to and derived from the present/future tense verb (الْفِعْل الْمُضَارِع - al-Fi'l al-Muḍāriʿ).

Important Rule

Before we begin, there's a vital rule in Arabic phonology:

"A word can never begin with a letter that's sakin (has a sukoon)."

Clarification: The first letter of a word must have a vowel (i.e., it has a Fatḥah ـَـ, a Ḍammah ـُـ, or a Kasrah ـِـ). It cannot start with a Sukoon ـْـ (which indicates the absence of a vowel).

How to Derive the Command Verb from the Present Tense Verb (for the Masculine Singular Addressee - أَنْتَ - you, masculine singular)

To form the command verb for a singular masculine person you are speaking to (أَنْتَ - anta), follow these steps, starting with the present tense verb (المضارع - al-Muḍāriʿ), usually in its "he" form:

Step 1: Remove the Present Tense Prefix

The present tense verb (Mudaari') begins with one of the four present tense prefix letters (ء/أ, ن, ي, ت). To get the command, we remove this prefix (typically the 'يـ' when starting from the base "he" form of the Mudaari').

  • يَقْرَأُ (yaqra'u - he reads) -> قْرَأُ (qra'u)
  • يَكْتُبُ (yaktubu - he writes) -> كْتُبُ (ktubu)
  • يَغْسِلُ (yaghsilu - he washes) -> غْسِلُ (ghsilu)
  • يَجْلِسُ (yajlisu - he sits) -> جْلِسُ (jlisu)
  • يَتَوَضَّأُ (yatawaḍḍa'u - he performs ablution) -> تَوَضَّأُ (tawaḍḍa'u)
Step 2: Dealing with an Initial Sukoon

If the first letter of the remaining word is sakin (has a Sukoon), we add a "connecting hamzah" (همزة الوصل - hamzat al-waṣl), written as an Alif (ا), at the beginning to enable pronunciation.

  • قْرَأُ (qra'u) -> (starts with sukoon on Qaf) -> ا + قْرَأُ
  • كْتُبُ (ktubu) -> (starts with sukoon on Kaf) -> ا + كْتُبُ
  • غْسِلُ (ghsilu) -> (starts with sukoon on Ghayn) -> ا + غْسِلُ
  • جْلِسُ (jlisu) -> (starts with sukoon on Jeem) -> ا + جْلِسُ
  • تَوَضَّأُ (tawaḍḍa'u) -> The 'ت' has a Fatḥah, so no Hamzat al-Waṣl is needed here.
Step 3: Determining the Vowel of Hamzat al-Waṣl

The vowel on the Hamzat al-Waṣl (the Alif: ا) depends on the vowel of the third letter of the original three-letter root of the verb (this is usually the second letter of the stem *after* removing the Mudaari' prefix and *before* adding the Hamzat al-Wasl).

  • If the third root letter has a ḍammah (ـُـ) in the Mudaari' form, the Hamzat al-Waṣl takes a ḍammah.

    يَكْتُبُ (yaktubu). The 'ت' has a ḍammah. So, the command starts with اُ (u). -> اُكْتُبْ (uktub).

  • If the third root letter has a fatḥah (ـَـ) or a kasrah (ـِـ) in the Mudaari' form, the Hamzat al-Waṣl takes a kasrah.

    يَقْرَأُ (yaqra'u). The 'ر' has a fatḥah. So, the command starts with اِ (i). -> اِقْرَأْ (iqra').

    يَغْسِلُ (yaghsilu). The 'س' has a kasrah. So, the command starts with اِ (i). -> اِغْسِلْ (ighsil).

    يَجْلِسُ (yajlisu). The 'ل' has a kasrah. So, the command starts with اِ (i). -> اِجْلِسْ (ijlis).

Step 4: Put a Sukoon on the End of the Verb

The command verb for a singular masculine addressee usually ends with a Sukoon (ـْـ).

Final Command Verbs (for singular masculine):

Mudaari' (Present) Command - Masculine English (Command)
يَقْرَأُ (yaqra'u) اِقْرَأْ (Iqra') Read!
يَكْتُبُ (yaktubu) اُكْتُبْ (Uktub) Write!
يَغْسِلُ (yaghsilu) اِغْسِلْ (Ighsil) Wash!
يَجْلِسُ (yajlisu) اِجْلِسْ (Ijlis) Sit!
يَتَوَضَّأُ (yatawaḍḍa'u) تَوَضَّأْ (Tawaḍḍa') Perform ablution!

Command Form for the Feminine Singular Addressee (أَنْتِ - anti - you, feminine singular)

To command a singular female, add Yā' al-Mukhāṭabah (ياء المخاطبة - the 'yaa' of addressing a female) to the end of the masculine command form. The letter preceding this 'ي' typically takes a Kasrah.

Masculine Command Feminine Command English (Feminine Command)
اِقْرَأْ (Iqra') اِقْرَئِي (Iqra'ee) Read! (to a female)
اُكْتُبْ (Uktub) اُكْتُبِي (Uktubee) Write! (to a female)
اِغْسِلْ (Ighsil) اِغْسِلِي (Ighsilee) Wash! (to a female)
اِجْلِسْ (Ijlis) اِجْلِسِي (Ijlisee) Sit! (to a female)
تَوَضَّأْ (Tawaḍḍa') تَوَضَّئِي (Tawaḍḍa'ee) Perform ablution! (to a female)
اِعْمَلْ (Iʿmal) اِعْمَلِي (Iʿmalee) Work! (to a female)
اُدْخُلْ (Udkhul) اُدْخُلِي (Udkhulee) Enter! (to a female)

Summary of Key Rules

  1. Start with the present tense verb - "he" form.
  2. Remove the present tense prefix - usually 'يـ'.
  3. If the resulting verb starts with a sukoon, add Hamzat al-Waṣl (ا).
    • The Hamzat al-Waṣl takes a ḍammah (اُ) if the third root letter of the verb has a ḍammah in the Mudaari'.
    • Otherwise, the Hamzat al-Waṣl takes a kasrah (اِ).
  4. Make the last letter of the verb sakin (ـْـ) for the singular masculine command.
  5. For the singular feminine command, add Yā' al-Mukhāṭabah (ي) to the end - usually with a kasrah on the preceding letter.

This framework will help you form command verbs for a wide range of common Arabic verbs. Practice is key to mastering these transformations!
